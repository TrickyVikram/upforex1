What Are Index CFDs and Why Trade Them?

An Index CFD (Contract for Difference) is a derivative product that allows you to speculate on the price movement of an underlying stock index without owning the actual assets. Instead, you enter into a contract to exchange the difference in the value of the index from the point the contract is opened to when it is closed. This method of CFD index trading offers distinct advantages over traditional investing, including the ability to use leverage, go short in falling markets, and gain exposure to an entire economy or sector through a single, streamlined position.
